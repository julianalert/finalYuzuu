Traditional product photography is expensive, slow, and logistically complex. You need a studio, a photographer, lighting equipment, props, and a post-production team. AI changes all of that. Today, any brand — from a solo founder to an enterprise team — can produce professional product photos in minutes. Here's how.

## Step 1: Prepare Your Source Product Image

The quality of your AI-generated photos depends heavily on the quality of your input image. A good source image should have:

- **Clean background:** A plain white or light-colored background makes it easy for the AI to isolate your product. If you only have a busy background, most AI tools (including Yuzuu) will automatically remove it.
- **Good lighting:** Avoid harsh shadows or overexposed areas. Even lighting from the front gives the AI the most information to work with.
- **High resolution:** Aim for at least 1000×1000 pixels. The higher the resolution, the better the final output.
- **Product fills the frame:** Don't leave excessive empty space around the product.

## Step 2: Choose Your Scene or Style

Once you have your source image, the next step is choosing what kind of scene you want to place your product in. Think about:

- **Who is your customer?** A fitness supplement brand needs different scenes than a luxury candle brand.
- **Where will the image be used?** Hero images on your website need different framing than thumbnail images on Amazon or Instagram ads.
- **What emotion do you want to evoke?** Trust and reliability call for clean, minimal studio shots. Aspiration and lifestyle need rich scene photography.

## Step 4: Review and Iterate

Not every generation will be perfect on the first try. Here's how to get better results through iteration:

- **If the product looks distorted:** The input image may have too much perspective distortion. Try re-shooting from straight on.
- **If the scene doesn't feel right:** Adjust your prompt. Add more specific details about lighting, color palette, or setting.
- **If the shadows look unnatural:** Try a different scene type. Some scenes are more forgiving than others with shadow placement.
- **If the image quality is low:** Check your source image resolution and ensure the product takes up most of the frame.
